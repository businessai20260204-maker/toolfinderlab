## Key Features: What Frase Actually Does

### SERP Analysis & Content Briefs

This is Frase's core feature and biggest time-saver. The workflow is straightforward: paste a target keyword into Frase, and it automatically analyzes the top 20 Google results for that keyword.

Within 30-60 seconds, Frase generates a content brief that includes:

**Common headings across top-ranking pages** ‚Äî Frase identifies which H2 and H3 headings appear most frequently in ranking content, helping you structure your article to match user intent.

**Frequently asked questions** ‚Äî Frase extracts questions from People Also Ask boxes and ranking content, which you can use to create FAQ sections or structure your article around common queries.

**Topic modeling** ‚Äî Frase identifies semantic keywords and concepts that top-ranking pages cover, helping you ensure comprehensive topic coverage.

**Competitor content summaries** ‚Äî Quick overviews of what each ranking page covers, saving you from reading full articles manually.

In my testing, Frase's SERP analysis saves 30-60 minutes per article compared to manual research. The briefs aren't perfect‚Äîsometimes Frase includes irrelevant headings or misses nuanced subtopics‚Äîbut they provide an excellent starting framework that you refine based on your unique angle.

**Comparison to manual research:** Manual SERP analysis involves opening 10-20 tabs, skimming each article, noting headings in a spreadsheet, and identifying patterns. Frase automates 80% of this process. The remaining 20%‚Äîunderstanding user intent and adding your unique perspective‚Äîstill requires human judgment.

**Comparison to Surfer SEO:** Surfer SEO's Content Editor provides more precise optimization scoring and real-time feedback as you write. Frase's briefs are faster to generate but less detailed. For speed, Frase wins. For precision, Surfer SEO wins.

### AI Content Writer

Frase includes an AI writing tool that generates article drafts based on your content brief. You provide an outline or select headings from the brief, and Frase writes paragraphs for each section.

**Honest quality assessment:** The AI Writer produces decent first drafts‚Äîbetter than writing from scratch, but noticeably below Surfer AI or Semrush ContentShake AI in quality. Frase-generated content tends to be generic and requires significant editing to add voice, examples, and depth.

In practical terms: Frase gets you 60-70% of the way to a publishable draft. You'll spend 30-40% of your time editing, restructuring, and adding personality. Compare this to writing from scratch (100% effort) or Surfer AI (80-85% quality, minimal editing needed).

**Best use case:** Use Frase AI Writer for sections where you're stuck or need to fill in background information quickly. Don't rely on it for introductions, conclusions, or sections requiring expertise‚Äîthese need human writing.

**What it's good for:** Explaining straightforward concepts, creating bullet-point lists, drafting FAQ answers, and generating first-draft structure.

**What it struggles with:** Original insights, compelling introductions, persuasive conclusions, and content requiring specific expertise or personal experience.

### Content Optimization & Scoring

As you write in Frase's editor (or paste in existing content), Frase provides a content score based on how well your article covers topics identified in the SERP analysis.

The optimization process works like this: Frase highlights topics and keywords you haven't covered yet, and your score increases as you incorporate them. The score isn't a guarantee of ranking‚Äîit's a measure of topic comprehensiveness compared to current top-ranking pages.

**My testing experience:** Frase's optimization scoring is helpful but less precise than Surfer SEO's Content Score. Surfer provides real-time feedback on keyword density, heading structure, and content length with specific targets. Frase's scoring is more general‚Äîit tells you *what* to cover but not precisely *how much*.

That said, for $15/month, Frase's optimization is genuinely useful. It catches gaps in topic coverage that I would have otherwise missed, and articles optimized with Frase consistently rank higher than those I write without any optimization tool.

**Practical workflow:** I use Frase to generate the brief and identify topics to cover, write the first draft in Google Docs, then paste it into Frase to check coverage and fill gaps. This hybrid approach works better than writing directly in Frase's editor.

### Question Extraction

Frase automatically pulls questions from Google's People Also Ask boxes and extracts questions from ranking content. This feature is underrated and incredibly useful for structuring content.

**How to use it:** Create an FAQ section using Frase's extracted questions, structure your article to answer these questions in H2 or H3 headings, or use questions as inspiration for subtopics you might have missed.

**SEO benefit:** Answering common questions improves your chances of appearing in featured snippets and Google's AI Overviews (formerly SGE). In 2026, question-focused content is increasingly important as AI search tools prioritize direct answers.
